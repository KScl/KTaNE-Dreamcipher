On the Subject of the Dreamcipher

Are you lucid enough to parse somebody else's dream?

  • Some of the glyphs on the module correspond to letters of the English alphabet; the other glyphs are decoys.
  • Determine the glyph alphabet using the steps below.
  • After determining the glyph alphabet, convert each glyph on the module back to a letter in order, treating outlined versions of glyphs the same as their filled counterparts. Ignore any glyphs that are not in the glyph alphabet.
  • The decrypted word will always be a valid English word between 8 and 15 characters in length.

Recreating the Glyph Alphabet

  • Start by creating a series of 16 bits from the glyphs shown on the module, where each bit is 0 if the glyph is outlined and 1 if it is filled.
    (The leftmost bit corresponds to the first glyph displayed.)
  • Take the leftmost eight bits, and convert them to a decimal number.
  • Use that number to determine the starting glyph, starting from 0 in the top left and proceeding in reading order. Wrap around if necessary.
  • Take the next four bits, and convert them to a decimal number.
  • Add one to that number, and advance that many additional glyphs, wrapping around if necessary. The resulting glyph corresponds to the last letter in the serial number.
  • Proceed likewise for the next four bits, and the next letter of the English alphabet.
  • If a glyph that has already been used is landed on, advance one more glyph.
  • Once all bits have been used, determine a new series of bits by doing the following:
    • Let x be the previous series of bits.
    • Shift x left by 1 plus the first digit in the serial number, XOR that value with x, and save the result as x.
    • Shift x right by 1 plus the last digit in the serial number, XOR that value with x, and save the result as x.
    • Shift x left by 1 plus (the sum of digits in the serial number, mod 15), and XOR that value with x. The result is the new series of bits.
  • Repeat the above operations until every letter in the English alphabet has been mapped to a glyph.
